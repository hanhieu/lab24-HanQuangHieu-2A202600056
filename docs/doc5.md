# Data Protection Officer (DPO) Requirements

## When a DPO is Required
A Data Protection Officer must be appointed when:
- Processing is carried out by a public authority
- Core activities involve regular and systematic monitoring of data subjects on a large scale
- Core activities involve large-scale processing of sensitive personal data
- Processing is high-risk as determined by impact assessment

## DPO Qualifications
The DPO should have:
- Expert knowledge of data protection law and practices
- Understanding of the organization's operations
- Ability to communicate with management and authorities
- Independence to perform duties without conflict of interest

## DPO Responsibilities
The DPO is responsible for:
- Advising the organization on data protection obligations
- Monitoring compliance with Decree 13/2023/ND-CP
- Providing training to staff
- Conducting internal audits
- Serving as contact point for data subjects and authorities
- Cooperating with supervisory authorities
- Maintaining records of processing activities

## DPO Independence
To ensure independence, the DPO:
- Cannot be dismissed for performing data protection duties
- Reports directly to senior management
- Has adequate resources and authority
- Is not involved in decisions about data processing purposes
- Can perform other duties only if they don't create conflicts

## DPO Registration
Organizations must register their DPO with the Ministry of Public Security within 30 days of appointment, including:
- DPO's name and contact information
- Organization's details
- Scope of DPO responsibilities
- Proof of qualifications
