# Cross-Border Data Transfer Regulations

## General Prohibition
Decree 13/2023/ND-CP generally prohibits the transfer of Vietnamese personal data to foreign countries unless specific conditions are met.

## Permitted Transfers
Cross-border data transfer is allowed when:
1. The receiving country has adequate data protection laws equivalent to Vietnam's
2. The data subject has given explicit consent
3. The transfer is necessary for performance of a contract
4. The transfer is for important public interests
5. The data controller has implemented appropriate safeguards

## Adequacy Assessment
The Ministry of Public Security maintains a list of countries with adequate data protection laws. As of 2024, this includes:
- European Union member states (GDPR-compliant)
- Japan
- South Korea
- Singapore
- Other countries with equivalent protections

## Safeguards for Non-Adequate Countries
For transfers to countries without adequate protection, data controllers must implement:
- Standard contractual clauses approved by authorities
- Binding corporate rules for multinational companies
- Codes of conduct or certification mechanisms
- Encryption and anonymization where possible

## Documentation Requirements
Data controllers must maintain:
- Records of all cross-border transfers
- Legal basis for each transfer
- Safeguards implemented
- Data subject consents (if applicable)
- Impact assessments for high-risk transfers
